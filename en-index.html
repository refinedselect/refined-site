Men’s Dress Shoe Brands Comparison

Refined Select is a guide to classic men’s dress shoes, built for readers who want to compare brands by country, style, fit, and overall character. This English page is a simple hub for exploring British, American, Italian, and French shoemakers.

What You Will Find Here

Men’s dress shoes vary greatly by country. British shoes tend to focus on structure and tradition, American shoes on comfort and practicality, Italian shoes on sleek elegance, and French shoes on refined design and distinctive lasts.

If you are new to classic footwear, start with the comparison and size guide pages below, then move to individual brand pages.

Brands by Country

United Kingdom

British shoemakers are known for classic styling, durable construction, and a more traditional feel.

  • Crockett & Jones
  • Church’s
  • Edward Green
  • John Lobb
  • Tricker’s
  • Joseph Cheaney
  • Loake
  • Grenson

United States

American dress shoes often balance practicality, comfort, and value. Many models feel more forgiving from the first wear.

  • Alden
  • Allen Edmonds
  • Florsheim
  • G.H. Bass
  • Johnston & Murphy
  • Cole Haan

Italy

Italian shoemakers often emphasize sleek silhouettes, elegant finishing, and a more fashion-forward appearance.

  • Santoni
  • Salvatore Ferragamo
  • A.Testoni
  • Bruno Magli

France

French shoemakers are often associated with refined elegance, distinctive lasts, and a subtle artistic touch.

  • J.M. Weston
  • Paraboot
  • Berluti

Popular Brands to Start With

If you are not sure where to begin, these brands are good starting points because they are widely discussed and represent different traditions.

  • Crockett & Jones – classic British balance
  • Alden – iconic American comfort and character
  • Santoni – elegant Italian style
  • J.M. Weston – refined French tradition

Beginner Guide

If you are buying your first pair of classic dress shoes, focus on three things first:

  • Construction: durable methods like Goodyear welted shoes are often easier to maintain long term.
  • Fit: brand sizing varies a lot, so comparison and sizing guides matter more than the number on the box.
  • Use case: decide whether you want formal office shoes, versatile business pairs, or more casual country styles.
